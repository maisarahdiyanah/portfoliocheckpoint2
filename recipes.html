Malaysian Snowball Cookies (Kuih Makmur)

Kuih Makmur, also known as "Kuih Momo" or "Snowball Cookies", is a traditional Malay dessert that is usually prepared during the Eid Al-Fitr festival. Growing up, this was one of my favorite cookies as a Malaysian celebrating Eid, and I still remember fighting for the cookie jar with my siblings just so I could finish all of the cookies in it. What I like about kuih makmur is that it has peanuts in it, and is not too sweet at all. This dessert is popular in Malaysia, Singapore, and Brunei.

Ingredients

  • 500g plain flour
  • 200g clarified butter (ghee)
  • Icing sugar to coat
    • Peanut filling
  • 200g roasted peanuts
  • 50g caster sugar

Method of Preparation

  1. Pre-heat oven to 190C.
  2. Add ghee to flour.
  3. Mix until all flour is coated with ghee and looks like breadcrumbs.
  4. Get a marble size dough and squeeze, if it holds shape then it is well mixed.
  5. Make a dent in the middle and fill with 1 teaspoon peanut filling.
  6. Enclose the filling with dough. Squeeze to shape.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes.
  8. Completely cool the cookies on a wire rack before dusting and coating with icing sugar.
